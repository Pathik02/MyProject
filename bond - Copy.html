BONDS

What are Bonds?

Bonds are fixed-income instruments that represent a loan made by an investor to a borrower, typically corporate or governmental. Bonds are used by companies, municipalities, states, and sovereign governments to finance projects and operations. Owners of bonds are debtholders, or creditors, of the issuer.

Types of Bonds

There are several types of bonds available to investors, each with its own characteristics:

  • Government Bonds: Issued by the government and considered low-risk investments.
  • Corporate Bonds: Issued by companies and generally offer higher yields compared to government bonds.
  • Municipal Bonds: Issued by states, cities, or other local government entities and often provide tax-free interest income.
  • Zero-Coupon Bonds: Sold at a discount and do not pay periodic interest but are redeemed at face value at maturity.
Benefits of Investing in Bonds

Investing in bonds offers several benefits, including:

  • Regular Income: Bonds pay interest, usually semiannually, providing a steady income stream.
  • Lower Risk: Bonds are generally less volatile than stocks and can provide a safety net during market downturns.
  • Diversification: Adding bonds to your investment portfolio can help diversify and reduce overall risk.
  • Capital Preservation: Bonds are a good option for preserving capital, especially for conservative investors.
